Part 0
Foundations
The why, the logic, and the operating architecture. This Part defines the purpose, philosophy, systems, and control model that underpin every business process in the handbook.
0.1 Purpose of this handbook

This Handbook defines the institutional operating system of Trimstone Partners. It governs how we design the firm (Build), operate the firm (Run), generate revenue (Consult), protect margin, control risk, and institutionalise knowledge.

This is not a process manual. It is the control architecture of the organisation. It defines the end-to-end process for creating, progressing, delivering, and closing Trimstone client work. It is written stage-by-stage and uses a consistent structure, enabling partners, finance, and operations to run deals and projects with clear controls.

0.2 How to use this handbook

1 Page = 1 Card = 1 Business Process = 1 Control Decision.

Each Card defines: Purpose · Risk prevented · Entry criteria · Required actions · Exit criteria · Financial impact · Controls & approvals · Escalation trigger · Owner · Linked systems (Torr, CRM, Xero).

Cards are designed to be clear, short (1–2 pages max), operational, and governable. If a process can't be clearly defined on a card, it isn't sufficiently structured or controlled.

0.3 Trimstone Operating Model

Trimstone operates as an integrated system. This cycle applies to every business process in this Handbook.

Build→ Run→ Consult→ Learn→ Reinvest

Build

Design the institutional architecture: governance, partner model, technology, finance structure, risk framework.

Run

Operate and control the architecture: finance cadence, risk & compliance, people & tech operations, partner oversight.

Consult

Monetise the platform: pipeline, pricing, contracting, delivery, close.

Learn & Reinvest

Capture IP, margin insights, and performance data. Strengthen platform and partner capability.

0.5 Economic control model

From client engagement to reinvestment — each stage adds, protects, or distributes value. Every business process must respect this economic flow.

Revenue→ Direct costs→ Gross margin→ Platform fee→ Partner economics→ Reinvestment

Every business process must answer: Does it protect the margin? Does it increase risk? Does it affect cash timing? Does it affect partner economics? If not, it is incomplete.

0.6 Governance philosophy

Every business process must clearly define: who is Responsible, who is Accountable, who Approves, who Escalates, and who bears the economic impact. No process exists without ownership.

Escalation is structural and pre-defined. Triggered by: financial deviation · contracting delays · compliance breaches · margin erosion · system misuse · governance non-adherence. Escalation is part of the system, not an exception.

In Trimstone, pipeline stages are not just tracking tools — they function as formal Decision Gates. No deal progresses without meeting defined Gate criteria. Movement between stages is a governance decision, not an administrative update.

Pre-deal requirements. Before any deal is created, two conditions must be met in Zoho CRM: the client organisation exists as an Account with priority set (H/M/L); relevant individuals exist as Contacts linked to the Account.

Part 0 · Section 0.4
Business Model & Value Proposition
Trimstone is a partner-led, governed consulting platform. Revenue is project-based. Margin protection is structural. Governance is embedded.
Business model — three structural pillars

Three structural pillars work together to generate revenue, protect margin, and control risk across every engagement.

Project-based
Revenue model
Revenue is project-based — no retainers, no vague relationships. The deal is captured in CRM and governed through pipeline stages.
Structural
Margin protection
Margin is set at pricing stage (v1–v4 governance) and monitored monthly. Structural controls — not willpower — prevent erosion.
Embedded
Governance
Commercial controls and decision rights are built into every process. Risk is managed structurally, not by ad-hoc judgement.
Value proposition — four forces

Value creation happens through structured interaction between four elements. No single element works in isolation — the system only functions when all four are active and aligned.

Partners
Senior partners own and deliver every engagement. Bring relationships and expertise. Bear commercial accountability and proposal costs.
Platform
Torr, CRM, Xero. Shared technology infrastructure. Enforces governance, pricing controls, and decision gates. Captures institutional knowledge.
Associates
Specialist and delivery capacity. Engaged via approved agencies (Barton, Eames, Tarka). Contracted under MSAs; onboarded to Torr and Xero.
Clients
Prioritised as High / Medium / Low in CRM. Access senior partner expertise on specific projects. Engaged through structured SOW-based contracting.
Value bridge — how value flows through the model
Client engagementRevenue is generated. A client commissions a specific project. Revenue is project-based — no retainers. The deal is captured in CRM and governed through pipeline stages.
Direct costsDelivery costs are tracked and controlled. Associate fees, subcontractor costs, and direct project expenses are booked against the project code in Xero. Tracked against pricing-sheet assumptions v1–v4.
Gross marginMargin is protected structurally. Margin set at pricing stage (v1–v4 governance) and monitored monthly during execution. Structural controls prevent erosion. Escalation triggers exist for deviation.
Platform feePlatform contribution is deducted. A defined platform fee is deducted before partner economics are calculated. Any variation to the standard fee requires Consulting MD sign-off.
Partner economicsReturns are distributed to the core partner group. Partner profit is calculated and distributed per the pricing sheet. On Closed Lost deals, proposal costs are reallocated across core partners. Economics are transparent and pre-agreed.
Part 0 · Section 0.8
System Integration Architecture
Trimstone operates through an integrated system built around Torr as the core workspace. Three systems work in concert across every deal stage.
Torr Portal — Operational core

Torr is the central workspace where the firm runs day to day. Anything client-facing or project-related lives here first.

  • Create clients, contacts, and projects
  • Live workspaces and document folders (auto-created at Prospecting & Scoping stage)
  • AI tools — TAIA, Proposal Generator, PPT Generator
  • Timesheets and expense linkage
  • Team-tab roster, project codes, Confidential folder
Zoho CRM — Commercial authority

Zoho is the source of truth for commercial status. All pipeline movement happens here.

  • Accounts (clients) with priority (H/M/L) — triggers Torr visibility
  • Contacts linked to Accounts
  • Deals — owned by Lead Partner; co-owners record proposal-cost sharing
  • Deal pipeline stages serve as Decision Gates
  • Final commercials, start/end dates, budget
Xero — Financial engine

Xero runs the firm's money. Set up only when the project is awarded; finance code must match the Torr project code.

  • Project financial setup (forecasts, partners, subcontractors)
  • Invoicing — client invoices dispatched on the 6th of each month
  • Expense submission (Xero Me) and approval (Hannah / Filip)
  • Margin tracking and partner settlements
  • Payment runs — 3rd and 17th of each month
ℹ
Finance codes in Xero must always match the project code created in the Torr Portal. This is the link that makes margin tracking and reconciliation possible.

Stage 1 of 8
In Discussions
🔒 Entry: Specific project opportunity identified
↗ Exit: Client requests further engagement
Purpose

Signals that a concrete project opportunity exists and is being discussed with the client. No commercial, contractual, or delivery commitments are implied.

Entry trigger. A specific project opportunity has been identified (not merely a priority relationship or target account). A deal is created and linked to an existing Zoho Account and Contact. Where an opportunity is new, the Account and Contact are set up alongside the Deal.

£
Financial treatment. All travel and related expenses are charged to the individual Partner's personal cost code. No reimbursement is made from personal codes at this stage. All costs should be recorded — they may be retrospectively allocated to the project if the deal progresses. If the deal stalls here: costs remain on personal codes; no reimbursement is made.

Stage 3 of 8
Pricing & Proposing
🔒 Entry: Preparing a formal or semi-formal proposal
↗ Exit: Pricing Sheet v4 MD sign-off + client email confirmation
Purpose

Creation, approval, and agreement of a commercial proposition for the client. Governed through the standard Trimstone pricing sheet — the single source of truth for commercials, profitability expectations, and downstream project setup.

Entry trigger. Trimstone is preparing a formal or semi-formal proposal (RFP, written proposal, or structured commercial discussions) and must define pricing, delivery structure, and commercial assumptions.

Pricing sheet version control
v1Partner draft — internal only. Prepared by Lead Partner. All mandatory partner input fields must be complete before submission to MD.
v2MD-approved — minimum for external sharing. Consulting MD approval required. Only version permitted to be shared with a client. Margin buffer confirmed.
v3Client-agreed version. Reflects agreed scope and commercials after negotiation. Email confirmation from client triggers v3 → v4 progression.
v4Final, MD signed-off. Triggers move to Contracting. Stored in the Confidential folder — MD access only.
£
Financial treatment. Partners should collect and may transfer legitimate prior personal expenses to the project code only at the Executing stage. Third-party expenses can be uploaded into Xero with a link to the Lead Partner code, clearly indicating that these are project-related expenses and specifying the project code. If the deal stalls here: Core Partners are not reimbursed; proposal and at-risk costs are allocated across the Core Partner team.
⚠
Escalate to Consulting MD if: margin below buffer · non-standard platform fee proposed · material scope change · commercial concession requested.

Stage 4 of 8
Contracting
🔒 Entry: Client award confirmed + MD sign-off on commercials
↗ Exit: Internal Consulting MD sign-off — max 2 weeks
Purpose

Formal contracting and full project setup once work is awarded and key terms have been signed off internally by the Consulting MD. Trimstone accepts that work and billing may begin at risk during this stage, but movement to execution should take place within 2 weeks of the start of any work. Move to execution requires internal sign-off to confirm contracting is complete.

⚠
Flag: We should never create a project in our pipeline from scratch at the Contracting stage. Instead, it should at least be added at the 'Prospecting & Scoping' stage, provided that the 'In Discussions' stage activities have been completed.
£
Financial treatment. Work and billing may begin at risk during 'Contracting'. Expenses incurred during this stage are collected and held — they cannot be formally assigned to the project code until the deal moves to 'Executing'. Partners and third-party team members should record all costs against their personal or Lead Partner code and tag them with the project code for later reallocation. If the deal stalls in 'Contracting': costs incurred at risk remain collectively borne by the Core Partner group.
⏱
2-week escalation rule. If a project remains in Contracting for more than 2 weeks after work has started, escalate to Consulting MD immediately. Review weekly until resolved.

Executing (Project Live)
🔒 Entry: Consulting MD internal sign-off from Contracting

Appendix A
Pricing & Proposal Sheet Process
The pricing process ensures that all Trimstone proposals are commercially coherent, internally consistent, explicit in their assumptions, properly governed, and usable for contracting, project setup, and execution.
Mandatory use

The standard Trimstone pricing sheet must be used for all proposals. No proposal may be shared externally unless a pricing sheet exists and has reached v2 (Consulting MD approved) at a minimum. One pricing sheet per deal.

Field classification
Pre-populated
System-derived — do not edit. Client name, project ID, derived dates, standard platform assumptions. Auto-populated from linked data.
Partner input (mandatory)
Project timing, delivery model (team, roles, suppliers), commercial assumptions (rates, scope boundaries, exclusions, caveats).
Calculated outputs
Do not override. Fees, revenue profile, costs, margins, partner economics. If outputs are unacceptable, adjust inputs — never override calculated fields.
Version control
v1Partner draft — internal only. All mandatory partner inputs must be complete before submission to MD.
v2MD-approved draft — minimum for external sharing. Margin buffer confirmed. Only version permitted to be shared with a client.
v3Client-agreed version. Reflects agreed scope after negotiation. Email confirmation from client triggers v3 → v4.
v4Final MD sign-off. Authoritative baseline for contracting. Permanent commercial record.
Stage movement

Deals remain in Pricing & Proposing through all four versions. Email confirmation from the client constitutes award and triggers v3 → v4. Approval of v4 triggers Deal status change to Contracting.

Storage

All pricing sheet versions and proposal artefacts must be stored in the project workspace on Torr. The v4 pricing sheet forms part of the permanent commercial record and must be in the Confidential folder.

Expense Policy
Guidelines for business-related expenses incurred by Trimstone consultants in accordance with UK tax regulations and HMRC requirements. Ensures consistent, fair, and compliant handling of all expense claims.
General principles
  • All expenses must have a legitimate business purpose
  • Expenses must comply with HMRC guidelines
  • VAT receipts required for all claims where applicable
  • Expenses must be submitted within 30 days of being incurred
  • Pre-approval required for expenses over £300
Travel

Rail. Standard class for journeys under 2 hours. First class permitted for longer journeys if pre-booked and approved. Advance booking required where possible. Railcard usage encouraged.

Air. Economy for domestic and European flights. Premium economy for flights over 6 hours. Book at least 14 days in advance where possible. London airports: use public transport where practical.

Ground transport. Use public transport when practical. Taxi/ride-share permissible when necessary. Car hire requires pre-approval.

  • Maximum rate per night (incl. VAT): £200 unless pre-approved
  • Must book through the approved corporate booking tool
  • Breakfast should be included where available
  • Personal expenses are not reimbursable

Client entertainment. Prior approval required. Max £100 per person. Business purpose must be documented. Full attendee list required. VAT receipt required. Must comply with the Bribery Act 2010.

Available codes (Xero Me)

When submitting expenses, you may allocate to:

  • Campaigns you are assigned to (e.g. Private Equity)
  • Your personal partner code (Partners only — for sales development; can later be transferred to a project)

Selecting project codes

The majority of expenses will be submitted using a client code. The majority of investments will be billed to partner codes.

⚠
Do not use a client project simply because you spoke to a client or because an expense might eventually lead to work. Unless the expense relates to an active, contracted project, it is business development and belongs on your partner code or your Lead Partner's partner code.

Even where a client project exists, if the expense is relationship-building rather than delivery, it may still belong on your partner code. Apply judgement, and consult with the Lead Partner. When submitting expenses to a partner code, use the non-billable codes.

Submitting expenses — Step-by-step

Step 1 — Main fields

  • Amount
  • Description — mandatory; must be clear and specific
  • Spent at — where applicable
  • Spent on — date of the expense
  • Account — select the correct account code
Use a standard (billable) account code when
The expense relates to delivery of an active, contracted engagement and the cost should be re-billed to the client.
e.g. travel to client site, hotel for project delivery, costs during delivery meetings.
Use a non-billable account code when
The expense is linked to developing the relationship on an active engagement and should not be re-billed. Client entertaining should always be coded as such, not as Accommodation & Meals.

Step 2 — Assign VAT. The account codes contain a default VAT amount (20% for Accommodation/Meals and General Expenses; 0% for Travel and Client Entertaining). For non-standard cases, adjust manually:

  • Zero-rated Accommodation and Meals abroad — set VAT to zero manually
  • Blended rate Meals out where service charge is added, or mixed VAT items (e.g. Pret hot takeaways vs. sandwiches) — itemise into two lines, with and without VAT

Step 3 — Assign a project (mandatory). Tap Optional fields → Assign to customer or project. Search for the client name or number and assign to the correct project. Do not assign to a customer. Leave Label and 'For accounts team only' fields blank.

⚠
Every expense must have a project assigned. Expenses without a project code will be rejected.
Project code naming convention

In Xero Me, project codes follow the format: [Client / Lead Partner] : [Project Name]

  • Tim Wright : Tim Wright — Tim's personal partner code
  • Tim Wright : Private Equity — a campaign led by Tim
  • NHBC : 10139-03 NHBC CTO Support — a specific client project under the NHBC relationship

Where a partner's name appears in both fields, this is their personal code. Where the second field differs, this is a client project or campaign.

Receipts, approval & rejection
  • A receipt is required for every expense submission
  • Any expense of £1,000 or more requires prior approval — contact the Lead Partner, cc'ing Hannah Fox or Filip Filev, before incurring the cost
  • Expenses without a receipt, or over £1,000 without prior approval, will be rejected
  • Credit card statements alone are insufficient

All expenses are reviewed and approved or rejected by Hannah Fox or Filip Filev. Expenses will be rejected if: no project/code selected · wrong type of code · description unclear/missing · no receipt · over £1,000 without prior approval · expense violates policy without prior approval.

Lead Partner responsibilities

The Lead Partner is responsible for reviewing all expenses charged to their project or campaign on a monthly basis:

  • Review all expenses charged to the project
  • Confirm whether each expense belongs to the project
  • Confirm whether each expense is billable or non-billable
  • Re-confirm that each expense is within policy
  • Review all expenses between £300 and £1,000 and ensure they were pre-agreed
  • Approve what is ultimately invoiced to the client where relevant

Payment Rules & Finance Cadence
Finance orchestrates monthly processes with the Lead Partner. Predictable, unconditional payment is a founding principle: ACs and suppliers are never penalised for Trimstone's own collections position with the client.
1 · Fee invoice payments (to ACs / suppliers)
  • Trimstone will always pay fee invoices on the 3rd of the month
  • This applies as long as the invoice was received by the 3rd of the previous month
  • Trimstone operates 30-day payment terms on fees — unconditionally
  • Even if Trimstone is still chasing the end client, the AC or supplier will still be paid on the 3rd
  • Trimstone absorbs the cash flow risk. ACs are never penalised for client delays
✓
